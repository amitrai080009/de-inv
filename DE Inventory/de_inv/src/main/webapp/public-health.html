What is Public Health and why is it Important?

Not many of us are aware of the term “Public Health” literally even when we have heard for a quite a few terms in our lives. Public Health in other words can be termed as “Community Health” and it refers to all standardized and efficient measures that are undertaken in order to prevent diseases in our society, improve the health conditions of the people and research about the injury prevention.

We cannot succeed in future if we forget about the failures we had in the past. Public Health practitioners try to imply some practical implications while learning with what went wrong before. The world is a complex place to live and hence there are great inequalities, health threats and risks. The need of the hour becomes to combat these existing and evolving risks.

Economic crisis, widening inequalities, ageing population, increasing levels of chronic disease, migration and urbanization are some of the challenges of Public Health in the 21st century as defined by World Health Organization (WHO).

Public Health does not confide to just one place. It holds importance and is relevant to all countries of the world- irrespective of their demographic features, economical abilities and their development stage. In other words from U.S.A. to Ghana, from Japan to Zambia all needs Public Health.

All over the world, Public Health programs work to improve the health and well being of communities by identifying risks that effect different people in different places at different stages of life and finding the best ways to minimise them. It’s about giving all the best chance to live long, healthy and fulfilling lives.

According to a survey, a child dies every three second due to some or the other infectious disease. But this can be prevented by promoting things like breast feeding, immunization (vaccination) and antibiotics. A lot of children across the world die because they do not have access to water facility and basic sanitation but public health care programs and improve that.

Public Health helps us to make right choices which help us now and in the future. Poor health does not only cost us life but also a lot of money. A healthier lifestyle can save us and a lot of cost of treatment. It’s about working to ensure that is no one is left behind as we strive for better.

Public Health professionals are not doctors. Doctors give us medicines to improve our health when we have already fallen ill. On the other hand, Public Health professionals do things that prevent us from falling ill. They promote health care equity, promote more improved, standardized and healthier lifestyles, conduct research, help in health administration, make educational programs and recommend various policies.

It is important to strengthen local, state and national healthcare departments in order to see a healthier population in near future. We have progressed a lot. The conditions aren’t same what they were a decade ago. But there is a lot that still remains to be achieved.

We need to understand what is lacking what makes us have a poorer Public Health than others. A lot can be done by just enforcing some laws more strictly such as: No smoking indoors or no driving without seatbelts, or helmets. All these laws need to be strengthened.
